Information
What is here?
The tables below show the buff each elixir gives, the buff level per item used, the duration each item adds, and the duration monster parts add to elixirs.

How to make:
Elixirs are made cooking small animals / critters with monster parts. Elixirs always need at least 1 critter and 1 monster part. This means the max amount of critters or monster parts that can be used is 4.

As long as at least 1 critter and 1 monster part is used normal ingredients, such as fish and mushrooms, can be added in.

Elixir Strength:
An Elixir's strength / potency / buff level is based solely off of the critters put in. While monster parts add duration, they have no effect on the strength. This means that Elixirs that have no duration (Hearty, Fairy Tonic, Enduring, and Energizing; your healing and stamina Elixirs) a low level monster part can be used without affecting the Elixir.

Elixir Duration:
Elixirs buff duration (how long it will last) is provided in minutes in the tables below. So 0:50 is fifty seconds and 1:30 is one minute and thirty seconds. With the exclusion of Hearty, Fairy Tonic, Enduring, and Energizing Elixirs, all elixirs have a duration.

Critical Elixirs:
Every time you cook an Elixir you have a chance to get a critical. You can guarentee a critical effect by cooking during a Blood Moon, using a dragon part, or using a Star Fragment. This can be noticed by additional sound / music when the item is revealed / cooked. The critical will add one of five effects.

+3 hearts restored
+1 temporary heart
+2/5 green or yellow stamina
+1 buff level (lvl 1 -> lvl 2, lvl 2 -> lvl 3)
+5:00 duration
